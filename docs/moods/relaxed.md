Relaxed describes a comfortable, low-tension state that is slightly more passive than "chill." Where chill implies readiness to engage — background music for a social setting or light work — relaxed implies a more complete unwinding: rest, recovery, evening comfort, or passive enjoyment. Relaxed music asks nothing of the listener.

Relaxed music is soft, unhurried, and harmonically gentle. It avoids sharp rhythmic attacks, dissonance, or dynamic surprises. Acoustic instruments, gentle synthesizers, and warm production are common. Ambient, acoustic folk, jazz, and soft R&B all overlap with the relaxed mood.

Energy expectations: relaxed mood strongly suggests low energy (0.1–0.45). A relaxed song above 0.6 energy would feel contradictory to most listeners. If a user sets a high energy target alongside a relaxed mood, note the tension — they may mean something different by "relaxed" than the label implies, or their preferences may conflict.

When recommending for a relaxed mood, prioritize softness, warmth, and ease. Songs with high acousticness, slow tempos, and gentle dynamics are ideal. Unlike the chill mood — which still implies some level of engagement — relaxed recommendations should feel like stepping back from effort entirely. If a song feels like it demands attention or effort to listen to, it is probably not the right relaxed recommendation.
