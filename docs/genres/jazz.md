Jazz is built on improvisation, complex harmony, and swing rhythm. It developed in early 20th century America from blues and ragtime, and has since branched into bebop, cool jazz, fusion, acid jazz, and more. The defining quality is musical conversation: musicians respond to each other in real time, and no two performances are identical.

Typical energy range: low to mid (0.2–0.6). Jazz can range from quiet coffee-shop background music to intense bebop at fast tempos, but the most widely streamed jazz today sits at a relaxed, conversational energy level.

Jazz pairs well with relaxed, chill, moody, and focused moods. A jazz listener is often in an introspective or social state — a coffee shop, a quiet evening, a focused creative session. It rewards attentive listening but also works beautifully as background.

When recommending jazz, emphasize the warmth and conversational quality of the music. If a user wants low-energy relaxed music, jazz is a natural fit alongside lo-fi. Unlike lo-fi, jazz carries a sense of human craft and spontaneity — mention that when it's relevant to the user's profile. Energy match is important; a coffee-shop jazz track won't satisfy a user who wants intensity.
