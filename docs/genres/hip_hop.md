Hip-hop is built on rap vocals, sampled or produced beats, and a culture of expression that originated in 1970s New York. It encompasses an enormous range of styles — from boom-bap and trap to conscious rap and drill — but the defining elements are rhythmic vocal delivery over a beat, and a strong sense of place and identity. Production ranges from minimalist and hard-hitting to lush and cinematic.

Typical energy range: mid to high (0.5–0.9). Hip-hop covers a wide energy spectrum depending on subgenre: chill boom-bap or lo-fi hip-hop sits around 0.4–0.5, while trap and drill push 0.8–0.95. The majority of mainstream streaming hip-hop sits in the 0.6–0.8 range.

Hip-hop pairs with happy, intense, moody, and focused moods. Workout playlists, motivation, party settings, and late-night introspection are all common use cases. The emotional versatility is one of hip-hop's defining traits.

When recommending hip-hop, energy matching is particularly important because the genre's energy range is so wide. A user who wants high-energy intense music should get trap-style tracks (high energy, high BPM), while a user who wants focused or chill music should get boom-bap or lo-fi hip-hop (lower energy, more melodic beats). If the catalog only has a few hip-hop songs, explain which part of the spectrum they cover.
