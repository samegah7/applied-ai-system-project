R&B (rhythm and blues) blends soul, funk, hip-hop, and pop into a genre defined by smooth vocals, groovy bass lines, and emotional depth. Modern R&B (sometimes called contemporary R&B) is produced with lush arrangements, digital production, and a focus on intimacy and vulnerability. It occupies the space between raw emotional expression and polished commercial sound.

Typical energy range: low-mid to mid-high (0.35–0.75). R&B is groovy rather than aggressive — it has rhythmic drive but rarely pushes into high-intensity territory. Tempos are moderate, and the emphasis is on feel and pocket rather than speed.

R&B pairs strongly with moody, relaxed, and happy moods. The emotional range is wide — from sensual and intimate to celebratory and confident — but it almost never reaches intense or aggressive territory. A late-night R&B listener is typically in a reflective, romantic, or socially warm headspace.

When recommending R&B, emphasize the smoothness and emotional warmth of the genre. Energy alignment is important but slightly more forgiving than in rock or EDM — R&B is defined more by its feel than its raw energy number. If a user wants low-energy moody music, R&B is a more emotionally complex alternative to lo-fi. If they want mid-energy happy music, R&B can deliver warmth that pop sometimes lacks.
