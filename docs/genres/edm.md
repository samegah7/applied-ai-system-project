EDM (electronic dance music) is designed for dancefloors and festival stages. It's built around a "build and drop" structure: a rising tension phase followed by a sudden release into a heavy bass drop. Sub-genres include house, techno, trance, dubstep, drum and bass, and future bass, but they share a commitment to high energy and rhythmic intensity.

Typical energy range: high to very high (0.75–1.0). EDM is consistently among the highest-energy music in any catalog. Tempos are fast (128–180+ BPM depending on sub-genre). Dynamic swings are dramatic — the drop hits much harder than the build.

EDM pairs almost exclusively with intense and happy moods. It's designed to produce euphoria on a dance floor, so happy intensity is its native emotional state. EDM rarely fits relaxed, chill, moody, or focused moods well — the energy and structure work against introspection or background listening.

When recommending EDM, be direct about its high-energy nature. If a user has a high target energy and an intense or happy mood, EDM is a strong fit — it will likely be the highest-scoring option in the catalog for that profile. If a user has conflicting signals (e.g., chill mood + high energy), EDM will score well on energy but be a genuine mood mismatch; note this tension rather than presenting EDM as unambiguously correct.
