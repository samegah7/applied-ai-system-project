Country music is rooted in storytelling, twangy guitar tones, and themes of rural life, heartbreak, freedom, and community. It originated in the American South and Appalachia and has evolved from classic honky-tonk and bluegrass through outlaw country to today's mainstream country-pop crossover. The common thread across eras is a strong narrative voice and an emphasis on emotional authenticity.

Typical energy range: low-mid to high (0.4–0.85). Country spans a wide energy range: a slow heartbreak ballad sits below 0.4, while a tailgate anthem can push 0.9. Mid-energy "driving song" country (0.5–0.7) is the most common style in modern streaming.

Country pairs naturally with happy, relaxed, and moody moods. Outdoor imagery, summer evenings, backroads, and small-town nostalgia dominate the emotional palette. Upbeat country feels celebratory; slower country feels nostalgic or bittersweet.

Important note for the recommender: if the catalog has limited or no country songs, users who prefer country will not receive a genre bonus on any song. When this happens, be honest — acknowledge that the genre they prefer isn't well-represented and explain that the recommendations are mood- and energy-based matches rather than true genre matches. This transparency is more helpful than pretending the results are perfect.
