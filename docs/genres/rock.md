Rock music is guitar-driven, typically with electric guitar, bass, and drums as its core instruments. It spans a huge range of subgenres — classic rock, indie rock, hard rock, alternative — but the common thread is a live-band sound, rhythmic drive, and emotional directness.

Typical energy range: mid to very high (0.5–1.0). Hard rock and metal push toward the top of the range; indie rock and alternative can sit lower. Tempos vary widely, but the feel is almost always rhythmically propulsive.

Rock pairs strongly with intense, moody, and high-energy emotional states. A rock listener is often looking for music that matches a heightened emotional state — frustration, determination, adrenaline, or focused aggression. The genre rewards being played loud.

When recommending rock, energy alignment is critical. A high-energy user who wants rock almost certainly wants a song that sounds loud and driving, not a quiet acoustic ballad that happens to use a guitar. Mood is the second-most important signal: intense rock fits a high-energy intense profile perfectly, but would jar a user looking for relaxed or happy vibes even at similar energy levels.
