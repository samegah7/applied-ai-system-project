Lo-fi (low-fidelity) music is defined by intentionally imperfect audio: vinyl crackle, tape hiss, soft background noise, and slightly muffled production. It emerged from bedroom producers who embraced technical limitations as an aesthetic. The sound is warm, nostalgic, and unpolished in a deliberate way.

Typical energy range: low to mid (0.2–0.5). Tempos are usually slow to moderate (60–90 BPM). Beats are often sampled or sampled-sounding, with jazz or soul influences underneath. Melodies are simple and repetitive, designed to fade into the background rather than demand attention.

Lo-fi is strongly associated with studying, late-night focus sessions, and quiet productivity. It pairs naturally with "chill" and "focused" moods. A user who requests lo-fi is usually looking for music that reduces cognitive load — something that fills silence without pulling focus away from a task.

When recommending lo-fi to a user, emphasize its role as a sonic environment rather than a performance. Songs with high acousticness, low energy, and chill or focused moods are ideal fits.
