Pop music is defined by broad accessibility, polished production, and a focus on melody and hooks. It draws from almost every other genre — rock, R&B, electronic, country — while filing off the rougher edges for mainstream audiences. The name itself comes from "popular," and chart performance and cultural reach are baked into what makes something "pop."

Typical energy range: mid to high (0.5–0.9). Tempos are usually upbeat (100–130 BPM). Song structures are predictable: verse, chorus, bridge, chorus. Production is clean, compressed, and loud-mastered for radio and streaming.

Pop pairs naturally with happy, excited, and high-energy moods. A pop listener is often looking for something immediately satisfying — a song that sounds good on the first listen and rewards repetition with a chorus that sticks.

When recommending pop, lean into the mood match above all else. Pop's defining quality is emotional directness: a happy pop song should feel unmistakably upbeat, not ambiguously pleasant. Energy alignment matters, but mood is the stronger signal for whether a pop recommendation feels right.
