Happy describes a high-valence, positive emotional state — upbeat, optimistic, and energized by good feeling rather than aggression or urgency. Happy music is celebratory and warm, characterized by major keys, upward melodic movements, bright timbres, and a sense of forward motion.

Happy differs from "intense" in important ways: happy is about pleasure and warmth, while intense is about drive and adrenaline. A workout track at energy 0.95 can be intense without being happy; a beach pop song at energy 0.7 is happy without being intense. Confusing the two is one of the most common mistakes in music recommendation.

Energy expectations: happy mood is compatible with a wide energy range (0.5–0.95) because happiness itself can range from relaxed contentment to euphoric excitement. However, very low energy songs (below 0.3) are rarely "happy" — they tend to feel peaceful, melancholic, or neutral instead.

When recommending for a happy mood, prioritize bright, warm, and positive-sounding tracks regardless of their genre. If a high-energy song is labeled "intense" rather than "happy," flag it as an imperfect match — it may satisfy the energy request but not the emotional one. A user who wants happy music should finish listening feeling uplifted, not just stimulated.
