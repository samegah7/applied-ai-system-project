Synthwave is a retro-futuristic electronic genre that draws heavily on 1980s film and video game soundtracks. It uses analog synthesizers, gated reverb drums, pulsing bass, and lush pads to evoke a cinematic, neon-lit aesthetic. The sound is simultaneously nostalgic and futuristic — designed to feel like a memory of a future that never happened.

Typical energy range: mid to high (0.5–0.85). The tempo is usually steady and driving (100–130 BPM), with a rhythmic pulse that propels forward motion without becoming aggressive. It's energetic but controlled — more "night drive" than "mosh pit."

Synthwave pairs naturally with focused, moody, and intense moods. It's strongly associated with late-night activity: late-night drives, coding sessions, gaming, and creative work after midnight. The aesthetic is cinematic and atmospheric, which makes it unusual among high-energy genres — it's intense without being loud or chaotic.

When recommending synthwave, lean into the night-drive, cinematic atmosphere. A user who wants high-energy focused music will find synthwave more satisfying than generic electronic or rock because it has momentum without distraction. If a user's profile has energy 0.7–0.9 and a focused or intense mood, synthwave is a strong fit even if they didn't specify it by name.
