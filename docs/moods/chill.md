Chill describes a low-arousal, positive emotional state — relaxed, comfortable, and at ease without being sleepy or disengaged. It's the dominant mood for background listening: music that reduces tension rather than building it. A "chill" listener is typically unwinding, socializing casually, or doing light tasks that don't require intense focus.

Chill music tends to have slow to moderate tempo, soft dynamics, and smooth tonal transitions. Harsh timbres, sudden loud drops, or aggressive rhythms all break the chill aesthetic. Production is typically warm and round rather than bright and sharp.

Energy expectations: chill mood strongly implies lower energy, typically 0.2–0.55. A song labeled "chill" with an energy above 0.7 is unusual and may not satisfy a user in this mood even if it matches technically. When a user asks for chill music but sets a high energy target, this is a tension worth noting — the mood and energy signals are contradicting each other.

When recommending for a chill mood, prioritize songs with smooth, unhurried sounds. Lo-fi, ambient, jazz, indie pop, and R&B all have natural chill subsets. High-energy genres like EDM or intense rock will almost always feel wrong for a chill request, regardless of numeric score.
